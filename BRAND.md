# CINEAST — Website Kit Foundation

## 1. Core Art Direction
**Brand mood:** cinematic, editorial, moody, intelligent, minimal
**Overall feel:** a fashion label crossed with an arthouse film journal
**Visual idea:** “a website that feels like a quiet opening scene”

This should not look like a loud movie merch store. It should feel:
- dark but refined
- spacious
- image-led
- typographic
- slightly nostalgic
- premium and slow

**Keywords:**
*Frame, still, grain, subtitle, sequence, midnight, archive, projection, scene, cut, memory.*

## 2. Design Language

### Color System

**Primary Palette**
- **Projection Black** — `#0A0A0A`
- **Screen Cream** — `#F2EEE8`
- **Dust Gray** — `#8A8781`
- **Silver Reel** — `#C6C2BB`
- **Oxblood** — `#5B1F26`

**Accent Palette**
- **Projector Amber** — `#C58B45`
- **Cinema Navy** — `#121A26`
- **Muted Olive** — `#5E6658`

> [!NOTE]
> **Usage:** Backgrounds should mostly be black, cream, charcoal, or deep navy. Oxblood and amber are accents only. Avoid bright colors unless for special campaign drops.

## 3. Typography System

Use a 3-font hierarchy.

**A. Editorial serif**
- **For:** hero headlines, journal titles, campaign statements.
- **Mood:** elegant, cinematic, literary.
- **Good style direction:** high-contrast serif, refined, slightly dramatic.

**B. Clean grotesk / sans-serif**
- **For:** nav, product names, body copy, buttons.
- **Mood:** modern, neutral, premium.

**C. Monospace or condensed utility font**
- **For:** timestamps, scene numbers, hover metadata, loading text, caption systems.
- **Mood:** technical, archival, subtitle-like.

**Typographic Behavior**
- Headlines should breathe.
- Large serif titles paired with small utility text feels very Cineast.
- Use all-caps utility labels sparingly: `SCENE 01`, `NOW SHOWING`, `ARCHIVE ENTRY`, `CUT TO:`.

## 4. Layout System

### Grid
- 12-column desktop grid
- Generous margins
- Long vertical rhythm
- Large image blocks with editorial spacing

### Section Rhythm
Alternate between:
- image-heavy sections
- quiet text-only sections
- split-screen layouts
- oversized quote/copy moments

### UI Treatment
- Thin borders
- Soft fade transitions
- Subtle hover reveals
- Almost no rounded playful UI
- Sharp, clean, controlled edges

## 5. Homepage / Landing-Page Structure

The landing page should feel like a sequence, not a store catalog.

**Recommended Flow:**
1. Loading page
2. Hero
3. Brand statement
4. Featured drop / collection
5. Editorial image strip
6. Journal / latest entries
7. Archive or manifesto block
8. Footer

## 6. Hero Section

**Goal:** Immediately communicate mood, brand identity, and aspiration.

**Layout Concept:**
Full-screen cinematic hero with:
- large background still/video loop
- transparent nav over top
- oversized serif headline
- small utility copy
- one primary CTA and one secondary CTA

**Hero Content Formula:**
- **Top utility line:** `SCENE 01 / NOW SHOWING`
- **Main headline options:**
  - *Make your life cinematic.*
  - *For people who watch closely.*
  - *Wear the frame.*
  - *Ordinary moments, directed beautifully.*
- **Supporting copy:** A cinematic lifestyle label built around mood, framing, memory, and the art of the scene.
- **CTA buttons:** `[Shop the Drop]`, `[Enter the Archive]`, `[Read the Journal]`

**Hero Visual Directions:**
- **Option A — City at night:** Rainy street reflections, soft grain, moving light, slow luxury.
- **Option B — Minimal studio:** Model in black tee / cap against cream backdrop with dramatic side light.
- **Option C — Film still collage:** Three slowly shifting stills: hallway, theater seat, apartment window.

**Hero Overlays:**
Use small metadata text:
`02:14 AM` | `LOCATION UNKNOWN` | `ARCHIVE ENTRY 001` | `SHOT IN LOW LIGHT`

## 7. Loading-Page

**Goal:** Make the brand feel intentional before entry.

**Look:** Black screen, centered wordmark, faint grain, slow fade, tiny utility text below.

**Motion Idea:**
- Wordmark fades in softly
- subtle flicker or projector pulse
- tiny progress indicator or loading line
- optional ambient dust/grain effect

**Best Version:**
Centered:
> cineast

Below in utility type:
> NOW SHOWING / PREPARING THE FRAME

At bottom corner:
> EST. 20XX / ARCHIVE SYSTEM ONLINE

## 8. Top Navigation

**Style:** Minimal, film-title-sequence energy.

**Structure:**
- **Left:** wordmark
- **Center or right:** navigation items
- **Far right:** cart / search / menu

**Nav Items:**
Shop | Editions | Journal | Archive | About
*(Optional: Search, Cart, Account)*

**Behavior:**
- Transparent over hero
- Turns solid black or cream on scroll
- Subtle blur background
- Thin bottom border appears on scroll
- Hover state reveals underline or tiny subtitle text

**Nice Detail:** On hover, each nav item can reveal tiny secondary labels:
- Shop → Latest Pieces
- Editions → Limited Drops
- Journal → Notes & Frames
- Archive → Past Scenes
- About → The Brand Story

## 9. Journal Section

This is very important because it makes Cineast feel like a world, not just a store.

**Role:** Editorial depth. It should feel like mood journal, archive notes, visual essays, scene studies, campaign storytelling.

**Layout Direction:** Use a magazine-like editorial layout:
- one featured story large
- two or three smaller entries beside or below
- image-led cards with lots of whitespace
- tiny metadata labels

**Section Heading Ideas:**
Journal | From the Archive | Notes on the Frame | Scene Studies | Screening Notes

**Entry Structure:**
- **Label:** `JOURNAL ENTRY 004`
- **Title:** Romance the Ordinary
- **Meta:** 4 min read / midnight notes / visual essay
- **Excerpt:** Thoughts on lighting, memory, and why some scenes stay with us longer than others.

**Journal Article Themes:**
- why grain still feels emotional
- cinematic dressing in everyday life
- the beauty of slow scenes
- a study of late-night city light
- objects that feel like film props
- frame, silence, tension, memory

**Visual Treatment:** large cover image, serif title, monospace metadata, thin borders, subtle grayscale hover-to-color shift.

## 10. Featured Collection Block

**Goal:** Bridge storytelling and commerce.

**Layout:**
Split screen:
- **Left:** product image or editorial campaign image
- **Right:** collection title, short poetic copy, CTA

**Sample Collection Names:**
Fade In | Midnight Screening | Archive Cut | On Location | Love Scene

**Sample Block:**
> FADE IN
> The opening sequence. Quiet staples for people who watch closely.
> `[Shop the Collection]`

## 11. Footer

The footer should feel like end credits.

**Style:** Dark, elegant, structured, subtle.

**Layout:** 4 columns or cinematic stacked layout.

**Column Ideas:**
- **01 / Brand:** Cineast statement, small mission line
- **02 / Navigation:** Shop, Journal, Archive, About
- **03 / Customer:** Shipping, Returns, Contact, FAQ
- **04 / Social:** Instagram, Pinterest, Newsletter

**Footer Copy Ideas:**
- End credits for the observant.
- Some scenes stay with you.
- Presented by Cineast.
- Quiet pieces for loud imaginations.

**Bottom Line:** Use small utility text: `CINEAST / ALL RIGHTS RESERVED / ARCHIVE SYSTEM ACTIVE`
Optional corner detail: `FADE OUT`

## 12. Background System

Backgrounds are a huge part of the mood.

**Primary Background Styles:**
- **A. Pure noir:** Flat near-black with subtle film grain. Best for hero, loading, footer, product focus.
- **B. Soft cream editorial:** Warm paper-like off-white with very faint texture. Best for journal pages, product descriptions, manifesto sections.
- **C. Deep navy cinematic:** Feels like late-night city light. Best for campaign pages and immersive sections.
- **D. Blurred still-image backgrounds:** Use defocused: theater lights, city reflections, windows in rain, projector beam, curtain folds. Very subtle. Never too busy.
- **E. Contact sheet texture:** A faint grid or repeated still motif for archive pages.

**Overlay Effects:**
- grain
- vignette
- soft fade-to-black
- thin frame lines
- subtitle-bar blocks
- timestamp overlays

> [!WARNING]
> **Avoid:** glossy gradients, loud neon effects, trendy startup blobs, generic luxury marble textures.

## 13. UI Motifs to Repeat Across the Site

To make the site memorable, repeat a few systems.

**Signature Motifs:**
- frame corners
- thin horizontal subtitle bars
- scene numbers
- timestamps
- archive labels
- opening/closing language: `FADE IN`, `CUT TO:`, `END CREDITS`, `NOW SHOWING`

**Repeating Utilities:**
`SCENE 01`, `ENTRY 004`, `RUN TIME`, `ARCHIVE ITEM`, `SHOT ON LOCATION`

*These small devices can appear in: product cards, journal entries, collection banners, page transitions, footer metadata.*

## 14. Motion Direction

Motion should feel slow, intentional, and filmic.

**Use:**
- fade-ins
- gentle slide reveals
- parallax on still imagery
- subtle grain flicker
- image zoom on hover
- wordmark drift or tracking animation

> [!WARNING]
> **Avoid:** bouncy animations, fast UI microinteractions, flashy transitions, overly playful movement.

**Think:** *“luxury editorial site with a projector soul”*

## 17. Best Overall Direction

If you want this site to feel truly strong, build it as:
**arthouse editorial minimalism + cinematic atmosphere + premium fashion structure**

That means:
- strong typography
- restrained color
- immersive imagery
- subtle motion
- journal depth
- product presentation that feels curated, not crowded

The site should feel less like “an online store” and more like:
**a brand archive you can buy from**
